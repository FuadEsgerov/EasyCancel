🇬🇧 United Kingdom

Your 14-day right to cancel a subscription in the UK

Bought a subscription online and changed your mind? UK law gives you a short window to walk away and get your money back.

Cooling-off period
14 calendar days
The law
Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013

When you sign up to a subscription online or over the phone, you enter a "distance contract". The Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013 give you a right to cancel within 14 days — often called the cooling-off period. The UK kept these rules after Brexit, so they still apply today.

When does the 14 days start?

For a service or subscription, the clock starts the day after the contract is concluded — usually the day after you sign up. You do not need a reason to cancel within this window.

What's covered — and the key exceptions

Most online services are covered, but there are important exceptions under Regulation 28:

  • Digital content (streaming, downloads, apps): if you asked to start immediately and ticked a box confirming you'd lose the cancellation right, you may no longer be able to cancel for a refund.
  • Services already fully performed: if you asked the service to begin and it's been fully delivered within the 14 days, the right can end.
  • If a service started during the period with your consent, the trader can charge you for what you actually used before cancelling.

New for 2026: Across the EU, a new directive requires online sellers to provide a clear "cancel" button by 19 June 2026. The UK is reforming its own subscription rules under the Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act — expect easier exits and clearer reminders before renewals.

How to cancel the right way

  1. Tell the trader clearly and in writing that you're cancelling — email or their cancellation form is fine.
  2. Do it within 14 days and keep a dated copy as proof.
  3. You should get your refund within 14 days of the trader being told.

Official source: legislation.gov.uk · Free help: Citizens Advice.

Common questions

Does the 14-day right apply to free trials?

The right relates to paid distance contracts. Once a paid period begins, the safest move is to track the start date and cancel before the next charge. EasyCancel does this automatically.

What if I already used the service?

You can still cancel within 14 days, but the trader may charge a fair amount for what you used before cancelling if you asked them to start early.

Is this different from just turning off auto-renew?

Yes. The cooling-off right can get your money back shortly after signing up. Turning off auto-renew only stops the next charge. Use the right tool for the timing you're in.
