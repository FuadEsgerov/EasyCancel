How to cancel Netflix the right way

Cancelling Netflix takes about a minute — but where you cancel depends on how you pay. Here's how to stop the next charge and keep watching until your period ends.

First, check how you pay

This is the step most people miss. If you signed up on netflix.com or an Android device, cancel through Netflix. If you subscribed through Apple (the App Store handles billing), you must cancel in your iPhone settings — cancelling on the Netflix site won't stop an Apple charge.

Cancel on the web (billed by Netflix)

  1. Go to netflix.com and sign in.
  2. Open the profile menu (top right) → Account.
  3. Select Cancel Membership and confirm.
  4. You'll keep access until the end of your current billing period — no partial refund, but no new charge.

Cancel if you're billed through Apple

  1. On your iPhone, open Settings and tap your name at the top.
  2. Tap Subscriptions.
  3. Tap Netflix → Cancel Subscription.

Just signed up? If you only subscribed in the last 14 days in the EU or UK, you may have a cooling-off right. See your country guide to check whether you can withdraw rather than just stopping the next charge.

Make sure it actually stopped

After cancelling, you should see a confirmation and an end date for your access. Watch your bank or App Store receipts next month to confirm no charge appears. EasyCancel can track the Netflix renewal date and remind you before it bills again.

Common questions

Will I lose access immediately when I cancel?

No. Netflix keeps your plan active until the end of the period you've already paid for, then it stops.

Can I get a refund for the rest of the month?

Netflix generally doesn't refund partial months. The value is in cancelling before the next charge — which is what reminders are for.

I cancelled on the website but was still charged. Why?

Almost always because billing runs through Apple. Cancel again under iPhone Settings › Subscriptions, then contact Apple about the charge.
