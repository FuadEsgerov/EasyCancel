How to cancel Spotify Premium

Cancelling Premium drops you to the free plan — you keep your playlists and saved music. Here's how to do it and stop the next charge.

First, check how you pay

If you subscribed on spotify.com, cancel through Spotify. If you subscribed through Apple, the App Store handles billing and you must cancel in your iPhone settings — cancelling on the Spotify site won't stop an Apple charge. (Note: Spotify often steers new sign-ups to web billing.)

Cancel on the web (billed by Spotify)

  1. Go to spotify.com/account and log in.
  2. Open Manage your plan → Change plan.
  3. Scroll to Spotify Free and choose Cancel Premium; confirm.
  4. Premium stays active until the end of your billing period, then you move to the free plan.

Cancel if you're billed through Apple

  1. On your iPhone, open Settings and tap your name.
  2. Tap Subscriptions → Spotify → Cancel Subscription.

Just signed up? If you subscribed in the last 14 days in the EU or UK, you may have a cooling-off right. Check your country guide to see whether you can withdraw rather than only stopping the next charge.

Will I lose my playlists?

No. Cancelling Premium switches you to Spotify Free — your playlists, saved songs and follows stay on your account. You'll have ads and shuffle limits, but nothing is deleted. EasyCancel can track your Premium renewal so you cancel before the next charge instead of after.

Common questions

Do I keep Premium until the end of the month?

Yes. You keep Premium features until the period you've already paid for ends, then you switch to free.

Will cancelling delete my account?

No — cancelling Premium only changes your plan. Your account, playlists and library remain.

I cancelled but was charged again. Why?

Usually because billing runs through Apple. Cancel again under iPhone Settings › Subscriptions, then contact Apple about the charge.
