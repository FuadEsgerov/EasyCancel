How to cancel Disney+ the right way

Cancelling Disney+ only takes a minute — but where you cancel depends on how you signed up. Here's how to stop the next charge and keep watching until your period ends.

First, check how you pay

This is the step most people miss. If you signed up directly on disneyplus.com or an Android device, cancel through Disney+. If you subscribed through Apple (the App Store handles billing), you must cancel in your iPhone settings — cancelling on the Disney+ site won't stop an Apple charge. The same applies if you got Disney+ bundled through a third party, such as your mobile carrier — you'd cancel with whoever bills you.

Cancel on the web (billed by Disney+)

  1. Go to disneyplus.com and sign in.
  2. Open your profile menu and go to Account.
  3. Find your Disney+ subscription and choose the option to cancel, then confirm.
  4. You'll keep access until the end of your current billing period — no partial refund, but no new charge.

Cancel if you're billed through Apple

  1. On your iPhone, open Settings and tap your name at the top.
  2. Tap Subscriptions.
  3. Tap Disney+ → Cancel Subscription.

Just signed up? If you only subscribed in the last 14 days in the EU or UK, you may have a cooling-off right that goes beyond simply stopping the next charge. Under EU and UK distance-selling rules you can usually withdraw from a new subscription within 14 days. See your country guide to check how it applies — and remember EasyCancel can track that deadline for you so it doesn't slip past.

Make sure it actually stopped

After cancelling, you should see a confirmation and an end date for your access. Watch your bank or App Store receipts next month to confirm no charge appears. EasyCancel can track the Disney+ renewal date and remind you before it bills again.

Common questions

Will I lose access immediately when I cancel?

No. Disney+ keeps your plan active until the end of the period you've already paid for, then it stops.

Can I get a refund for the rest of the month?

Disney+ generally doesn't refund partial periods. The value is in cancelling before the next charge — which is what reminders are for.

I cancelled on the website but was still charged. Why?

Almost always because billing runs through Apple or a bundle partner. Cancel again under iPhone Settings › Subscriptions (or with the partner that bills you), then contact them about the charge.
