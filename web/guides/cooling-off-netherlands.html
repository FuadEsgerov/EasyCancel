Herroepingsrecht: 14-day right to cancel in the Netherlands

Subscribed online and want out? Dutch law gives you a herroepingsrecht — 14 days to withdraw without giving a reason.

Withdrawal period
14 days
The law
Art. 6:230o, Burgerlijk Wetboek

A subscription taken out online or by phone is a distance contract (overeenkomst op afstand). Under Article 6:230o of the Burgerlijk Wetboek, you have a herroepingsrecht — 14 days to withdraw (ontbinden) without giving a reason.

When does the period start?

For a service contract, the 14 days (the bedenktijd) run from the conclusion of the contract. If you weren't informed of the right, the period can be extended by up to 12 months.

What's covered — and the exceptions (Art. 6:230p)

  • Digital content not on a physical medium: the right is lost if performance began with your express prior consent and acknowledgment that you'd lose it.
  • Services fully performed with your prior express consent can fall outside the right.
  • If a service started at your request during the period, you may owe a proportionate amount.

New for 2026: EU Directive 2023/2673 introduces a mandatory online "opzeggen" button, applying from 19 June 2026. Leaving an unwanted subscription is getting much easier.

How to withdraw correctly

  1. Send a clear statement of withdrawal (herroeping) to the trader — email, the model form, or letter.
  2. Do it within 14 days and keep dated proof.
  3. Refunds are due within 14 days of your withdrawal.

Common questions

Does it apply after a free trial converts to paid?

The right attaches to the distance contract. Track the paid start date and act quickly — EasyCancel flags the deadline and the next charge.

What if I wasn't told about my right?

If the trader didn't inform you, the bedenktijd can be extended by up to 12 months. Keep your confirmation email.

Is herroeping the same as opzeggen?

No. Herroeping unwinds a fresh contract within 14 days; opzeggen ends an ongoing subscription for the future.
