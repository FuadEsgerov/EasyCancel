Cancel the right way — before you're charged again.

Across the EU and UK, you usually have 14 days to withdraw from an online subscription. These plain-English guides explain your rights by country and how to cancel popular services.

New for 2026: EU Directive 2023/2673 requires online sellers to add a clear "cancel your contract" button. Member states must apply the rules from 19 June 2026 — making it easier than ever to leave a subscription you no longer want.

Your cooling-off rights by country

Pick your country to see how the 14-day withdrawal right works, which law protects you, and the common exceptions to watch for.

United Kingdom

14-day right under the Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013.
🇩🇪

Germany

🇫🇷

France

Droit de rétractation — Article L221-18 du Code de la consommation.

Italy

Netherlands

Herroepingsrecht — Art. 6:230o, Burgerlijk Wetboek.
🇵🇱

Poland

Prawo odstąpienia — Art. 27, Ustawa o prawach konsumenta.
