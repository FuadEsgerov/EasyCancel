How to cancel Audible the right way

Cancelling Audible takes a few minutes — but where you cancel depends on how you signed up. Here's how to stop the next charge and keep the books you already own.

First, check how you pay

This is the step most people miss. If you signed up on Audible's or Amazon's website, cancel through your Audible account on the web — the cancellation flow generally isn't available inside the mobile app. If you subscribed through Apple (the App Store handles billing), you must cancel in your iPhone settings — cancelling on the Audible site won't stop an Apple charge.

Cancel on the web (billed by Audible/Amazon)

  1. Go to audible.com (or your local Audible site) in a desktop browser and sign in.
  2. Open Account Details from your account menu.
  3. Find your membership and choose the option to cancel, then follow the prompts to confirm.
  4. You'll keep access until the end of your current billing period. Any audiobooks you've already bought or redeemed with credits stay yours to keep, even after cancelling.

Cancel if you're billed through Apple

  1. On your iPhone, open Settings and tap your name at the top.
  2. Tap Subscriptions.
  3. Tap Audible → Cancel Subscription.

Just signed up? If you only subscribed in the last 14 days in the EU or UK, you may have a cooling-off right that goes beyond simply stopping the next charge. Under EU and UK distance-selling rules you can usually withdraw from a new subscription within 14 days. See your country guide to check how it applies — and remember EasyCancel can track that deadline for you so it doesn't slip past.

Make sure it actually stopped

After cancelling, you should see a confirmation and an end date for your membership. Watch your bank or App Store receipts next month to confirm no charge appears. EasyCancel can track the Audible renewal date and remind you before it bills again.

Common questions

Do I keep my audiobooks after I cancel?

Yes. Audiobooks you've purchased or redeemed with credits stay in your library even after your membership ends.

What happens to unused credits?

Unused credits typically expire when your membership ends, so it's worth spending them before you cancel.

I cancelled but was still charged. Why?

Almost always because billing runs through Apple. Cancel again under iPhone Settings › Subscriptions, then contact Apple about the charge.
