Diritto di recesso: 14-day right to cancel in Italy

Subscribed online and changed your mind? Italian law gives you a diritto di recesso — 14 days to withdraw without giving a reason.

Withdrawal period
14 days
The law
Art. 52, Codice del Consumo

A subscription taken out online or by phone is a distance contract (contratto a distanza). Under Article 52 of the Codice del Consumo (D.Lgs. 206/2005), you have a diritto di recesso — 14 days to withdraw without justification and without penalty.

When does the period start?

For a service contract, the 14 days run from the conclusion of the contract. If you weren't informed of the right, the period can be extended.

What's covered — and the exceptions (Art. 59)

  • Digital content not on a physical medium: the right is lost if performance began with your express prior consent and acknowledgment that you'd lose it.
  • Services fully performed with your prior express consent can fall outside the right.
  • If a service started at your request during the period, you may owe a proportionate amount.

New for 2026: EU Directive 2023/2673 introduces a mandatory online "annulla / disdici" button, applying from 19 June 2026. Leaving an unwanted subscription is getting much easier.

How to withdraw correctly

  1. Send a clear statement of withdrawal (recesso) to the trader — email, the model form, or letter.
  2. Do it within 14 days and keep dated proof.
  3. Refunds are due within 14 days of your withdrawal.

Common questions

Does it apply after a free trial converts to paid?

The right attaches to the distance contract. Track the paid start date and act quickly — EasyCancel flags the deadline and the next charge.

What if I wasn't told about my right?

If the trader didn't inform you, the withdrawal period can be extended (up to 12 months plus 14 days). Keep your confirmation email.

Is recesso the same as disdetta?

No. Recesso here unwinds a fresh distance contract within 14 days; disdetta ends an ongoing subscription for the future.
