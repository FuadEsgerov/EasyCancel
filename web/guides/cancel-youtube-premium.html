How to cancel YouTube Premium the right way

Cancelling YouTube Premium takes about a minute — but where you cancel depends on how you signed up. Here's how to stop the next charge and keep your benefits until your period ends.

First, check how you pay

This is the step most people miss. If you signed up on the web or an Android device, cancel through your Google/YouTube account. If you subscribed through Apple (the App Store handles billing), you must cancel in your iPhone settings — cancelling on YouTube won't stop an Apple charge.

Cancel on the web (billed by Google)

  1. Go to youtube.com and sign in.
  2. Open your account menu and select Purchases and memberships.
  3. Find YouTube Premium and choose Deactivate or Cancel membership, then confirm.
  4. You'll keep your benefits until the end of your current billing period — no partial refund, but no new charge.

Cancel if you're billed through Apple

  1. On your iPhone, open Settings and tap your name at the top.
  2. Tap Subscriptions.
  3. Tap YouTube Premium → Cancel Subscription.

Just signed up? If you only subscribed in the last 14 days in the EU or UK, you may have a cooling-off right that goes beyond simply stopping the next charge. Under EU and UK distance-selling rules you can usually withdraw from a new subscription within 14 days. See your country guide to check how it applies — and remember EasyCancel can track that deadline for you so it doesn't slip past.

Make sure it actually stopped

After cancelling, you should see a confirmation and an end date for your benefits. Watch your bank or App Store receipts next month to confirm no charge appears. EasyCancel can track the YouTube Premium renewal date and remind you before it bills again.

Common questions

Will I lose access immediately when I cancel?

No. YouTube keeps your Premium benefits active until the end of the period you've already paid for, then they stop.

Can I get a refund for the rest of the month?

YouTube generally doesn't refund partial periods. The value is in cancelling before the next charge — which is what reminders are for.

I cancelled but was still charged. Why?

Almost always because billing runs through Apple. Cancel again under iPhone Settings › Subscriptions, then contact Apple about the charge.
